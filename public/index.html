Principles

  1. Start with a simple test at the API-level of your unit under test.
  2. Without using mocks, work from the outside to the inside of your unit under test. Reduce the scope of a unit under test instead by using stubs that pretend to have solved one or more subtasks of the unit under test.
  3. Grow tests and implementation slow and steady by writing the obvious implementation, fake it 'till you make it and triangulate.
  4. During development, treat your suit of tests as append only. Do not delete or comment out a test that is formally correct, but conflicts with your current development state. Instead, revert to a state where you can make continuous progress again.
  5. If there are stubs in the unit under test, replace the stubs with their real implementation by making them the new unit under test and go to step 1.
  6. Always add a validation test after each unit under test has been finished to avoid overfitting to the training set that you used to develop the unit under test.
